## Configure your WordPress site
Once you have your admin account for WordPress all set up (previous setup), you can proceed to visit your new dashboard.      
Just go to www.yoursite.com/wp-admin/ and input the access details for your admin account. Once you log in, you‚Äôll see this:    

![wordpressadmin](/assets/images/wordpress-dashboard.jpg)       


### General settings
Start by going to the left sidebar and clicking on `Settings ‚Üí General`.       

![wordpresssetup](/assets/images/wordpress-general.jpg)       

And this is where you can set:     

- Site Title:      
Effectively the name of your site.       
The title is particularly important since it‚Äôs what every visitor sees first, and also a big part of how search engines determine your rankings.       
Make sure that you have your business name there.     
- Tagline:       
Think of it as the summary of what your blog is about, in one short sentence.       
For example: ‚Äú{{ site.description }}‚Äù

In this section, you can also check if your blog address (URL) is in order, set the timezone you‚Äôre in, and your preferred language.       
Another important thing to note is that Bluehost has a Coming Soon feature in the last section of General Settings. Once you‚Äôre ready to go live with your blog make sure to turn this feature off.       

### Permalinks
The ‚Äúpermalinks‚Äù settings define how WordPress will go about creating individual URLs ‚Äì webpage addresses ‚Äì for each of your individual blog posts or sub-pages.        
Though the idea might sound a bit advanced, setting this is very simple.         
Just go to `Settings ‚Üí Permalinks`.        
Once there, set your permalinks to the ‚ÄúPost name‚Äù variant, like so:      

![wordpresspermalinks](/assets/images/wordpress-permalinks.jpg)        

This is the most optimal setting of the ones available and also the one preferred by Google and other search engines.      

### Visibility
The next, super-important thing that you must do is make sure that search engines such as Google can index your new blog.     
To do that, go to `Settings ‚Üí Reading` (again, from the main sidebar) and make sure that the field labeled ‚ÄúDiscourage search engines from indexing this site‚Äù remains unchecked.      
Like so:

![seo](/assets/images/wordpress-seo.jpg)       

If you don‚Äôt do that, your blog might end up literally invisible to Google and many other Search Engines.

### Add new post
